About Me...

Hello! I'm Jihee, a junior double majoring in Statistics and Computer Science at the University of California, Berkeley.

My academic interests lie in using statistics, computer science, and machine learning to build statistical models and applying data science to bring meaningful discoveries in industry and research. I am currently looking for opportunities in actuarial sciences and data science. ​For the past three years, I spent my summers building statistical models with UC Professors and PhD students making forecasts of public health projects. This summer, I served as the data science lead for ING Start-up and created MVA and ranking system models which were voted upon, built by the software engineers, and used in the final product submitted to Y Combinator. Currently, with Professor Scott Lee of the Berkeley School of Public Health, I am using statistics and machine learning towards medical eye-research in the Bay Area and also work as an academic development mentor for Berkeley's Data Science Society.

I believe in using data science, statistics, and computer science to emerge new discoveries that spark value in our world. I am looking to practice applying my knowledge in the actuarial and data science fields. Here you will find projects and experiences I have explored at Cal. Enjoy!
